The World Has One Harvest

The Problem

Although The Gambia has the climate and soil conditions to be a large producer of agricultural goods, several factors suppress these farmers’ food production. Gambian farmers have little market access, which suppresses food production. As a result, they produce food that is both nutrient deficient and less than ideal for export. In addition, the country imports nearly all of its food. These factors make farmers and communities in The Gambia malnourished, disenfranchised, and dependent on foreign nations. Common Harvest has found that these farmers are producing to a fourth of their potential.

The Solution

Common Harvest aims to fulfil its mission to build a cooperative supply chain to help aggregate, ship, and market fair trade produce for subsistence farmers, while partnering with micro-finance organization and local agriculture extension agents to ensure farmers have the necessary resources to gain the greatest return for their efforts.

From the initial blueprint to the boots-on-the-ground work, Common Harvest has impacted some lives across the Gambia.
